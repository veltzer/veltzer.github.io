# Blog Post Ideas

A running backlog of post ideas. The recent batch shares a temperament:
reality is stranger and harsher than our consoling stories; we
systematically flatter ourselves; the uncomfortable truth is the more
impressive one. Move items to "Done" (with the post slug) as they ship.

## Proposed

### Science indicts / fiction consoles
- **Introspection is a press secretary, not a president.** No privileged
  access to your own mind — you confabulate reasons after the fact
  (split-brain, choice blindness, Nisbett & Wilson). The "inner self that
  knows itself" is a comforting fiction. *(science, philosophy)*
- **The self is a useful illusion.** No central "you" runs the show; the
  unified, continuous self is a story the brain stitches together.
  Buddhism stumbled near this; neuroscience nailed it. *(science, philosophy)*
- **Free will as the last consoling story.** The one piece of
  self-flattery almost everyone refuses to drop, and why determinism plus
  the indifference of physics make it untenable — and why that's fine.
  *(philosophy, science)*
- **Your morality is mostly reputation management.** Much of what feels
  like pure conscience is evolved machinery for being *seen* to cooperate.
  Companion to the Kant/game-theory post. *(science, game-theory)*

### Constrained imagination beats free imagination
- **Mathematics is more imaginative than any mythology.** Non-Euclidean
  geometry, infinities of different sizes (Cantor), Gödel — ideas no
  storyteller could invent, forced on us by rigor, not freedom.
  *(philosophy, science)*
- **Prediction is the real magic.** The genuine miracle isn't a burning
  bush — it's Mendeleev leaving gaps in the periodic table for elements
  not yet discovered, then having them turn up with the predicted
  properties. Religion has no equivalent. *(science, philosophy)*

### Religion
- **Why theology produces no discoveries.** In 2,000 years, has any
  theological "method" ever found out a single new true thing about the
  world? Contrast with the discovery rate of one decade of science.
  *(religion, science)*
- **The argument from embarrassment, inverted.** Apologists cite
  "embarrassing" Gospel details as evidence of truth; but every cult and
  conspiracy generates embarrassing details too. Awkwardness isn't
  evidence. *(religion)*

### New angles (same temperament, fresh territory)
- **The anthropocentric conceit.** Every pre-scientific worldview puts
  humans at the center — of the cosmos, of creation, of moral concern.
  Every scientific advance has been a *demotion* (Copernicus → Darwin →
  neuroscience → cosmology). The pattern is the tell. *(science, philosophy)*
- **Consolation is not evidence.** A belief feeling comforting (afterlife,
  justice in the universe, "everything happens for a reason") is
  *negatively* correlated with its being true, because comfort and truth
  are optimized by different processes. A clean general principle that
  underwrites half the other posts. *(philosophy, religion)*
- **Heritability is the truth nobody is allowed to say.** The most
  socially radioactive findings in science (behavioral genetics) — why the
  taboo exists and why the discomfort is, again, the tell. The spiciest;
  decide how hard to push. *(science, opinion)*
